About Us

Who Are We?


ASTRONOMY, a natural science, is the study of celestial objects and aboutes, the physics, chemistry, and evolution of such objects and aboutes, and more generally all phenomena that originate outside the atmosphere of Earth. So we at LNMIIT, Jaipur made a club for the astronomy enthusiasts to quench their thirst for knowledge here only.

We are a bunch of people enthusiastic towards astronomy. Fascinated with the mysteries of the night sky and majestic views of telescope.

The LNMIIT Astronomy Club was founded by Y10 Batch students : Rishabh Baid, Rajat Vyas, Samarth Mathur and Shasak Raina. The club aims to create awareness in the field of amateur astronomy by indulging in night-sky observations, talks and various competitions.


What We Do?


“Astronomy compels us to look upwards and leads us from this world to another.” - Plato.

Astronomy is not limited to theoretical concepts but involves practical observation of what lies beyond our horizon. It questions the origin and existence of our universe. This keeps us driving and entices the desire to learn.

The club has successfully built an environment of healthy exchange of knowledge about astronomy and astrophysics. The dedicated team has made the college proud by winning various astronomical competitions held across the nation. We aim higher!

ARMAGEDDON

Date : 18th - 19th Jan 2017
Venue : The LNMIIT, Jaipur
×

Synopsis

Scientists at NASA have discovered an asteroid 65 miles wide, named as Destroyer786. It is composed of large quantities of carbon molecules as well as the more usual rocks and metals.It has it's collision course set with Earth. The asteroid is supposed to make its impact 1.5 years from today. Destroyer786 is travelling at a speed of 30,000 miles per hour and is approaching the earth from the direction of the sun. Keeping the estimated flyby distance in mind, it is supposed to cause a mass destruction if not total wipeout of humanity from the face of Earth.
All countries have come together, understanding the grim situation and are ready to take extreme measures to stop the 'end of the world'. You are chosen to be a part of core team of scientists that is going to save the humanity.
Devise a plan to save Earth and humanity. Fate of humanity lies in your hand. This event consists of two rounds. First round will be online. The participants have to submit a report before the given deadline. After the date of submission, we will select the teams for the second round on the basis of their report. All the teams selected teams will have to present their report in front of the judges during plinth.


Event Description

Problem Statement

  • You have no economic constraints as all countries are willing to pool their resources in an attempt to save humanity.
  • You have 547 days in total to save earth, delay of even an hour will lead to human extinction.
  • Level of technology is not much advance than today, therefore make assumptions that are almost realistic.
The final project report should be mailed to astronomy.lnmiit@gmail.com with the subject:
Plinth: Armageddon Project Report: TeamId.


Rules

General Rules

  • Any form of copying of textual material would lead to disqualification.
  • You can work alone or in a team of at most five.
  • One project should be submitted by only one team/individual. If same project is submitted by two teams/individuals, first one would be evaluated and second would be disqualified.

Format of the project

  • The project should be submitted in a pdf.
  • There is no strict format that you need to stick to. Make it as comprehensible as possible.

Judging Basis

Evaluation Methodology

  • Your report would be evaluated on how many different aspects you have deliberated upon and how many of these are in line with the physics of the planet.
  • The more descriptive you are, the more points your report will score.
  • You can use diagrams and pictures.

ASTRO HUNT

Date : 18th - 19th Jan 2017
Venue : The LNMIIT, Jaipur
×

Synopsis

Foresee the unfathomable and explore the mysteries of the universe. Only the sharp, intelligent and astronomy enthusiasts will make it to the end. The route to solve the mysteries of the universe lies within the depths of the dark sky and your imagination. It’s time to bring out the Galileo Galilee in you and look into the infinite cosmic depths.


Event Description

  • At the beginning of the Astro Hunt each team will be given a map of the LNMIIT Campus and a Sky chart.
  • Astro Hunt will consist of 4 rounds.
  • At the beginning of each round every team will receive an identical set of clues or puzzles which must be solved in order to find a piece of information. This information could be words from a historical astronomical event, name of a star, planet or any other celestial body, cosmic theories or just a name of a person. The clues and puzzles will be related to astronomy in some manner and will lead to a location/check point within walking distance where players will use a vital piece of information to answer a question.
  • After completing a round by solving the puzzles/clues and reaching the check point the teams will get a flag and the clues/puzzles for the next round.
  • Each flag will also consist of some information/clue. After collecting all the 4 flags the participants will have to solve the final puzzle.
  • The team which will collect all the 4 flags and solve the final puzzle in minimum time will be the winner.

Rules

General Rules

  • Members per team: 3 -5
  • Each team should have unique participants i.e. no two teams can have even one common name registered
  • The participants have to reach the starting point 15 minutes before the event schedule.
  • All the team members should be present at each check point while receiving a token.
  • Decision of the volunteers at each check point will be final.
  • Usage of Internet is allowed.

STAR TREK

Date : 18th - 19th Jan 2017
Venue : The LNMIIT, Jaipur
×

Synopsis

This event consists of 2 rounds: Spacewalk and Messier marathon. Detailed description of the event are as follows.

1st Round: Spacewalk

It will be an audio-visual quiz related to astronomy.Pictures and videos would be projected in front and participants have to answer the questions that follow.

2nd Round: Messier Marathon

So are you one of those who sneak out of your rooms during night hours and wander long gazing at the sky, trying to build up your imagination. Well folks then this is a perfect game for you to play, a hunt for the stars. Participants need to set themselves on a voyage of different celestial bodies and conquer to best of their capabilities. So, are you ready for this mission to get kicked off? See you at the battle field . A basic tutorial session would be held on handling of telescope and any further enhancements of the skills would be student’s personal interest.


Event Description

Spacewalk

  • Participants will have 1 minute to answer each question by writing it down on the given sheet.
  • These questions will be objective or one word.
  • Each question will carry 2 points.
  • Marks will be allotted to each question individually.
  • Only top 50% teams will go to the next round.
  • Teams will be provided with certain token numbers and called upon on its basis.
  • A star map, a laser pointer and telescope shall be provided to the team
  • The teams which had qualified in the quiz would be required to point at certain celestial objects through pointer and telescope
  • Minimum time shall be provided, based on which points are to be rewarded.
  • Team with maximum score would be declared winner.
  • Teams are suppose to restrain themselves from using any android application such as Google Sky Map or any other lectronic media.
  • Points will be given for locating the objects precisely only on first attempt.

Rules

General Rules

  • Participants can form team of up to three.
  • Individual participants are also allowed.
  • Using mobile phones is not allowed.
